Collect the prepared insert parameter tuples in a list and call cursor.executemany(...) once instead of executing c.execute(...) inside the loop. Also open the SQLite connection with a context manager (with sqlite3.connect(...) as conn:) so the connection is properly committed/closed even on error. Expected effect: reduces SQLite round-trips and transaction/lock overhead for many rows (better performance and lower risk of DB locking), and ensures the DB connection is always closed (improved resource cleanup). This is a small, low-risk change: replace the per-row c.execute() with building params = [] then c.executemany(insert_sql, params) and remove the explicit conn.commit(), or keep it if desired.
