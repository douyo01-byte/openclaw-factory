Add a small input-size guard (e.g. MAX_HTML_LEN = 200_000) and truncate the html argument at the start of extract_emails, extract_text_urls, and extract_a_links when len(html) exceeds that limit. This prevents pathological or very large pages from causing expensive, long-running regex scans or excessive memory/CPU usage. Expected effect: reduces risk of timeouts and CPU spikes when processing extremely large responses with minimal chance of missing common inline contacts/URLs near the start of the document.
